It was Monday, May 4, 2026, around 1:07 PM, when Carlos Ramirez, a brilliant Spanish physicist living at 265 Hayden Lane in North Carolina, stumbled upon destiny in his dusty basement. Amid cobwebs and forgotten lab equipment, he unearthed an ancient, musty notepad. Its yellowed pages were scribbled with what appeared to be math equations at first glance. But as Carlos pored over them under the dim bulb light, a chilling truth emerged: the fundamental laws of physics weren't natural discoveries—they were guarded secrets, hoarded by the U.S. Army.

Fueled by obsession, Carlos embarked on a grueling five-hour drive to the nearest military base. Tires humming on the interstate, his mind raced with quantum possibilities. Upon arrival, he demanded answers from stone-faced officers. "The laws of physics—where do they truly originate?" But the Army stonewalled him, citing "national security." Defeated, Carlos endured a torturous one-day drive home, snarled by endless traffic jams under a relentless sun.

What awaited him was apocalypse. His modest home lay in smoldering ruins, courtesy of a rogue nuclear facility encroaching on the neighborhood. The air reeked not of acrid smoke, but bizarrely, of grilled hotdogs—perhaps from a nearby vendor fleeing the chaos. Heart pounding, Carlos rummaged through the debris and retrieved his most prized invention: the Defyn 2x000 ring. This unassuming band could transform anyone into a brutally honest ten-year-old version of themselves, stripping away adult deceptions.

Slipping it on, Carlos confronted the facility's security forces. "Who destroyed my home?!" he bellowed. The guards, now wide-eyed kids in oversized uniforms, spilled the truth: "We're hiding physics secrets for the government! The real laws are buried in the Earth's core!" But honesty made them clumsy; the "grumpy facility" itself seemed to rebel, its automated defenses stumbling like toddlers learning to walk. Time warped around it—perhaps a side effect of the ring—devouring the structure in a swirl of chronal decay. Workers gawked in confusion, Carlos included.

Shifting strategy, Carlos zapped the workers. They babbled childlike confessions: "The ultimate physics truth is at the planet's heart—a giant red orb holding every equation!" Chastising himself with fifty self-inflicted spanks for his earlier naivety ("Foolish adult thinking!"), Carlos seized a chainsaw from the wreckage. Revving it wildly, he plunged downward, burrowing uncontrollably through earth layers—soil, rock, magma—in a mad descent.

Hours blurred into eternity until he breached the center. There, suspended in void, floated a colossal red giant ball, pulsing with otherworldly energy. As Carlos approached, it didn't explode or repel—it Rickrolled him. Blasting from invisible speakers: "Never gonna give you up..." Carlos's jaw dropped. "WHAT!?" he exclaimed.

Then it hit him. The ball wasn't a weapon; it was the universe's ultimate prank—a cosmic Rick Astley hologram encoding the laws of physics in meme form. Every equation danced in the lyrics: gravity in the beat, relativity in the rhythm. The Army knew, the facility guarded it, the notepad hinted at it. Laughing maniacally, Carlos synced his Defyn ring to the orb, downloading the truths into his mind.

Emerging from the core, chainsaw spent and clothes singed, Carlos returned to the surface a changed man. He exposed the secrets, rickrolling governments worldwide until physics became open-source. The world never looked—or sounded—the same. And Carlos? He traded his lab coat for a trench coat, forever the physicist who drilled to the Earth's heart... and got never gonna give it up.
