BIG RED SUN / RICKROLLING BALL — NOTEPAD THEORIES (ADDENDUM)

These theories are written like cautious field notes. They don’t prove anything.
They just explain what Carlos *might* have been seeing.

I. “ESCAPE NOT” AS A SAFETY WORD
It could be that “escape not” is not a command to stay.
It might be a warning that the basement will only let you leave if you do it in a way your mind can’t simulate.

When Carlos tries the “back door,” the notepad might switch from story-mode into lock-mode.
That would explain why the world turns blank right after the attempt.

II. THE “40%” ISN’T A PERCENT.
It could be a ratio of two frames of reference.
Like: the scene is partially correct, but not fully aligned.

If your brain tries to finish the missing alignment, it might “render” the wrong world state.
That would make people melt after understanding—because understanding is what triggers the final alignment.

III. THE SUN IS A CLOCK WITH FEWER LEVERS
The giant red sun might represent a solver, not a star.
Stars are steady on human timescales, but solvers are not.

So “As the earth ages the sun was always in the earth in the form of a liquid blob” could mean:
- the solver never leaves the system
- it only changes shape
- and the only thing that changes is your interpretation of where “up” is

IV. ABOVE/BENEATH AS “NEGATED COORDINATES”
It could be that “What is above and below where they are” describes an inversion.
Not a literal above/below.
More like: if the notepad says “above,” the real variable is “negative depth.”

When you try to answer the inversion, your internal map might flip.
If the flip completes, the basement can “lock in” the wrong map.
That might be why the escape story ends in melting.

V. “FLIES OF DARKNESS” AS A MEMORY-JAM SYMBOL
It might be that the “flies of darkness” are not creatures.
They could be repeating patterns in the notepad ink.
Small repeated instructions.

If you keep reading them, they could loop your predictions.
Then your mind gets stuck predicting the same next line.
Eventually you stop controlling the prediction.

When that happens, the body might feel like it’s dissolving.
But it’s really your perception losing control first.

VI. “NOR ANSWER” IS THE BASEMENT’S WAY OF BLOCKING COMPLETION
The sun “did not dwelt. Nor answer.” could be intentional.
If the system answers, the equation completes.
If the equation completes, the basement locks.

So the basement might avoid direct replies.
Instead it provides partial riddles until your brain stops asking questions.
That matches the earlier margin line: LEVEL 3 is a moment you stop.

VII. RICKROLLING BALL AS AN “ENCODER / DECODER” METAPHOR
It could be that “rickrolling” is just the narrator describing a decoy protocol.
A protocol designed to look like it should lead somewhere.

If you follow the decoy, you enter a tunnel that returns you to the same state.
The state changes only in your mind.
Eventually your mind realizes too late that it’s not advancing.

That would explain “Just figuring out the equation … was suggestion of a world filled of blank.”

VIII. “NOR SUGGEST WHY WE ARE MELTING” AS A CONTROL SIGNAL
Not suggesting why could be the point.
If Carlos knew the exact cause, he could avoid it.

So the notepad might hide the causal chain on purpose.
It might present effects (melting) and interpretations (sorrow, loneliness, escape not)
without giving a direct lever.

If there is no lever, there is no safe completion.

FINAL NOTE (ARCHIVE HANDWRITING):
In other words, the notepad might not want you to solve.
It might want you to *agree* with the solver.
And agreeing is the dangerous part.
